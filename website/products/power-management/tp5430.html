TP5430 Low Dropout Linear Regulator

500mA Output Current • 15µA IQ • 85dB PSRR • Ultra-Low Noise

Product Overview

The TP5430 is an ultra-low quiescent current, low-dropout linear regulator designed for noise-sensitive applications requiring clean power rails. With only 15 µA of quiescent current, it is ideal for battery-powered and portable applications where power efficiency is critical. The device features an extremely low dropout voltage of 85 mV at 100 mA, maximizing the usable battery life.

This LDO regulator provides exceptional line and load regulation with high power supply rejection ratio (PSRR) making it ideal for powering RF and analog circuits that require clean supply voltage. The device is stable with ceramic capacitors as small as 1 µF and features internal protection including current limiting, foldback protection, and thermal shutdown.

Field Application Engineer Insights

When designing with the TP5430, proper PCB layout is essential for optimal performance. Use a star ground connection and place a 1µF or larger ceramic capacitor as close as possible to both input and output pins. For applications requiring the lowest noise, consider adding an additional 10µF tantalum or ceramic capacitor at the output.

The excellent PSRR of the TP5430 makes it ideal for powering RF circuitry and analog front-ends. For maximum PSRR performance, ensure that the input voltage is adequately filtered before reaching the LDO. The enable function allows for power sequencing in multi-rail systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is dropout voltage and why is it important?

Dropout voltage is the minimum difference between input and output voltage required to maintain regulation. A low dropout voltage allows the LDO to maintain regulation as the input voltage approaches the output voltage, maximizing the usable capacity of batteries. The TP5430's 85mV dropout at 100mA is exceptionally low, allowing longer battery life.

How does PSRR affect RF performance?

Power Supply Rejection Ratio (PSRR) measures the LDO's ability to reject noise from the input supply. For RF applications, high PSRR is crucial to prevent switching noise from DC-DC converters from reaching sensitive RF circuits. The TP5430's 85dB PSRR at 1kHz provides excellent isolation for RF applications.

Can I parallel TP5430 devices to increase output current?

Multiple TP5430 devices can be connected in parallel to increase output current capability. Connect them with small resistors in series with each output to promote current sharing. Use separate input and output capacitors for each device to ensure stability. When paralleling, ensure proper thermal management for the combined heat dissipation.
