TP5450 Integrated Battery Management IC

Li-Ion/Li-Po Charger • Fuel Gauge • Protection • 5A Charge Current

Product Overview

The TP5450 is a highly integrated battery management solution that combines battery charge management, fuel gauging, and battery protection functions in a single IC. This device is specifically designed for single-cell Li-ion and Li-polymer battery applications in portable devices like smartphones, tablets, power banks, and wearable devices.

Featuring a switch-mode battery charge controller with high efficiency and low heat dissipation, the TP5450 provides precise charge management using a constant-current/constant-voltage algorithm. The integrated fuel gauge uses impedance tracking technology to accurately predict the remaining battery capacity over the entire life of the battery. Comprehensive protection functions safeguard the battery from overcharge, over-discharge, overcurrent, and short-circuit conditions.

Key Features

  • Switch-Mode Charger (Efficiency >90%)
  • Charge Current: Up to 5A (Programmable)

Field Application Engineer Insights

When implementing the TP5450 in your design, ensure proper layout for the high-current paths. Use wide traces and multiple vias for the battery and input connections to minimize resistance and inductance. The thermal regulation feature will automatically reduce charge current if the die temperature exceeds 120°C.

The fuel gauge algorithm requires characterization data for the specific battery pack being used. This information needs to be programmed into the device's memory during manufacturing. Pay special attention to the PROG resistor as it sets both the full-scale current for the fuel gauge and the charge termination current.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Impedance Track technology?

Impedance Track is a fuel gauging algorithm that tracks the battery's impedance as it ages, allowing for accurate capacity prediction throughout the battery's life. It measures the voltage drop under load to calculate the battery's internal resistance, which correlates to the state of health (SoH) of the battery. This is more accurate than simple voltage-based fuel gauging algorithms.

How does the battery protection work?

The TP5450 provides multiple layers of protection. It monitors battery voltage for over-voltage and under-voltage conditions, current for over-current conditions, and responds to short-circuit events. When a protection event occurs, the device disconnects the battery from the load or charger through internal FETs. The protection thresholds are programmable through the I²C interface.

Can I use the TP5450 with different battery chemistries?

The TP5450 is specifically designed for single-cell Li-Ion and Li-Polymer batteries. It is not suitable for other battery chemistries like NiMH or lead-acid. Using it with incorrect battery chemistries can result in safety hazards or poor performance. For other chemistries, use battery management solutions designed specifically for those types.
