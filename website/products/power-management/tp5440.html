TP5440 Multi-Channel Power Management IC

5 Integrated Regulators • Power Sequencer • Voltage Monitors • Watchdog Timer

Product Overview

The TP5440 is a highly integrated power management IC designed for complex system power requirements such as those found in FPGAs, processors, and SoCs. The device integrates five voltage regulators (three buck converters and two linear regulators) along with power sequencing, voltage monitoring, and general-purpose I/O functions to simplify system design and reduce BOM cost.

With its integrated power management capabilities, the TP5440 enables designers to implement sophisticated power management schemes with minimal external components. The device supports flexible power-up/down sequences, provides real-time voltage monitoring, and includes safety features like reset outputs and a watchdog timer to ensure reliable system operation.

Key Features

  • Three Synchronized Buck Converters (up to 2A each)
  • Two LDO Linear Regulators (up to 200mA each)
  • Power-On and Power-Off Sequencing
  • Voltage Monitoring with Programmable Thresholds
  • Reset Generator with Manual Reset Input
  • Watchdog Timer with WDI Input
  • GPIO Pins for General Purpose I/O
  • Internal Ring Oscillator
  • Supply Range: 3.0V to 20V (buck input), 3.0V to 5.5V (LDO input)
  • Package: QFN-48

Target Applications

  • FPGA Power Supply Systems
  • Processor Power Management
  • SoC Power Delivery
  • Communications Infrastructure Equipment
  • Industrial Control Systems
  • Automotive Electronic Control Units

Field Application Engineer Insights

When implementing the TP5440 in FPGA or processor applications, carefully plan the power sequencing to meet the requirements of your target processor. Many SoCs and FPGAs have strict voltage ramp-up and power-down requirements that must be adhered to prevent device damage.

The integrated voltage monitors can be programmed to different thresholds to suit various system requirements. For systems with complex power requirements, utilize the GPIO pins for additional control functions like external FET control or status indication. The high degree of integration significantly reduces external component count compared to discrete solutions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is power sequencing and why is it important?

Power sequencing refers to controlling the order, timing, and voltage levels during power-up and power-down of multiple rails in a system. This is critical for devices like FPGAs and processors that have specific requirements for which voltage rails must reach their target values before others. Incorrect sequencing can cause device malfunction or permanent damage.

How does the watchdog timer function in the TP5440?

The watchdog timer requires the host processor to periodically send a pulse to the WDI pin to prevent a system reset. If the host fails to send a pulse within the timeout period (1.6ms or 100ms selectable), the device asserts the reset output. This ensures system reliability by resetting the processor if it becomes stuck or unresponsive.

Can I configure the output voltage values?

Yes, the output voltage for all regulators can be configured. The buck converters and LDO regulators have adjustable outputs using external resistors for feedback. The device also supports fixed output voltage options for simpler designs. The voltage monitors can be programmed to specific threshold values for system supervision.
